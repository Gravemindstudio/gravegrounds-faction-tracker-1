Faction Lore

When the Veil shattered, the world bled. And where that blood seeped into the earth - the Swarm was born. It began in the low places: the mires, forgotten graves, and plague-pits no priest dared to sanctify. The first were accidents: a drowned child splitting open with a clutch of chittering larvae, or a battlefield rotting into a nest of bone-wasp and flesh-flies.

No necromancer had cast spells here. The Veil's breaking had awakened the decay itself. And it remembered how to hunger. The Swarm does not simply kill. It recycles. Every death is a birth. Every grave is a cradle. They do not fight fair. They fight biologically. They out-breed, out-rot, outlast.

Abilities & Powers

  • Organic Necromancy: Cast spells with bile, blood, and disease
  • Plague Glyphs: Sow fields with runes that hatch into carnivorous moss
  • Flesh-Wasp Hives: Summon swarms to strip armies to bone in hours
  • Hunger Hexes: Cause defenders to devour their own kin
  • Infection Breeding: Turn every corpse into a new horror

Leadership

The Rotmother

She does not rule. She gestates. Buried deep in the belly of the Mirefang Swamps, a rotted mound of flesh the size of a cathedral heaves with every breath. Her skin is veined with black eggs, her mouth a ruin of teeth and birthing ducts.

The Unbirthed Queen

Territory

The territories of the Swarm are impossible to cleanse. The trees weep pus, the rivers thicken with hatch-flesh, and the air buzzes with the wings of unborn things. Villages fall without battles: first the wells are tainted, then grain is seeded with plague, then infants cry with two mouths. By the time an army marches into the Mireborn's lands, they are already dead - they just don't know it yet.

Faction Philosophy

"You bury one. We birth a thousand. We are not mindless. We are guided by the Rotmother's hive-mind, by Mirelords who were once human sorcerers and warlords. Our necromancy is not clean. It is organic, rotting, alive. We cast spells not with words, but with bile, blood, and the squirming scripts of disease. We do not break kingdoms. We digest them."
